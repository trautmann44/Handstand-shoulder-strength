# Handstand Shoulder Strength Project

## Overview
This project investigates factors influencing handstand stability in gymnasts, with a focus on shoulder strength and training experience.

## Background
Shoulder flexor and extensor strength is often considered important for gymnastic performance, particularly for maintaining handstand stability. However, stability may also depend on other factors such as training experience.

## Aim
The aim of this study was to examine the influence of:
- isometric strength ratio of shoulder flexors and extensors  
- years of experience  
- experience level (national vs. international)  

on handstand stability.

## Methods
- **Participants:** 19 individuals  
- **Task:** 30-second handstand  
- **Measurement:**
  - Stability assessed via center of pressure (CoP) displacement using a Footscan RS pressure plate  
  - Shoulder strength measured with VALD ForceFrame  
- **Analysis:** Linear model with handstand stability as the dependent variable  

## Results
- **Years of experience** was the only significant predictor of handstand stability  
- **Strength ratio** was not significant  
- **Experience level** (national vs. international) was not significant  

## Conclusion
Handstand stability appears to be primarily influenced by experience rather than shoulder strength. This suggests that it is a complex motor skill that develops mainly through practice.
